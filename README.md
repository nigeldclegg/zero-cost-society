# The Zero Cost Society

An open, evolving body of ideas about how human life could be organised without money at its centre.

Not a political movement. Not a business. A commons of practical thinking that anyone can read, use, improve, translate or fork.

## Why this exists

Money is not free. Every pound made is made from something and taken from somewhere, and the ledger the world keeps shows only one side of the entry. There is always another side. All money comes at a cost, to someone, or to something, or to both.

That cost is now visible everywhere: in exhausted land and seas, in work that produces things designed to fail, in people who are hungry where there is food and lonely where there are people everywhere.

The interesting question is not whether this is bad. It is whether it is necessary.

Three things have changed. We can grow far more food in far less space. Energy from sun and wind is now cheaper than energy from fuel. Machines can make most physical goods at almost no marginal cost. Scarcity was the problem money was invented to manage. Where scarcity is engineered away, money becomes a tool for a job we have better tools for.

This project collects, in plain language, what a society organised on that basis would actually require. Not slogans. Specifics.

## What is here

Concepts, one domain at a time: food and growing, transport, energy, housing, health, environment, waste, making, knowledge, resource allocation, governance.

Each is written to the same shape: what the concept is, why the current system prevents it, evidence that it works, what is still unsolved, and the honest objections with honest answers.

That last part matters most. A project like this earns trust by hosting its own best counterarguments, not by hiding them.

See `SCHEMA.md` for the format and `CONTRIBUTING.md` for how to take part.

## What is not here

No plan for seizing anything. No prediction of collapse. No claim that this is easy, or fast, or that every question has been answered. Many have not. Those are written down too.

## The licence, and why

Everything here is under **Creative Commons Attribution ShareAlike 4.0**. Copy it, change it, translate it, print it, build on it. No permission needed.

ShareAlike means anything built from this must stay equally free. Nobody can take this work, improve it, and lock the improvement away. That is not a legal footnote. It is the whole idea, written as law: the one thing that cannot be done to this commons is enclosure.

## How this grows

Nobody has to be in charge for this to work.

This repository is one curated version. Suggest changes and they may be accepted or not. Disagree with the direction and you are free to fork it and continue your own, with no permission and no ill feeling. Translations, adaptations and rival editions are all welcome outcomes.

The project does not depend on any individual continuing to care about it.
