# Concepts

The body of the work. Each page covers one domain and follows the same seven-part shape — what it is, why the current system prevents it, how it works, what cannot be written down, evidence, unsolved questions, and objections.

See [SCHEMA.md](../SCHEMA.md) for the format and [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md) for how to add or change a page.

## Start here

**[Foundations](foundations.md)** — the base argument, the six conditions a moneyless community appears to require, and the objections that apply to the whole project. Read this first. Every other page assumes it.

## Domains

**[Food & Growing](food.md)** — feeding a community from close to where it is eaten. Controlled-environment growing, outdoor polyculture, seed keeping. The strongest evidence base of any domain here.

**[Energy](energy.md)** — generation, storage and distribution at community scale. The cost curves that make the whole argument possible, and the supply-chain contradiction that undercuts it.

**[Transport](transport.md)** — moving people and goods without ownership. Shared driverless vehicles on the surface, automated goods tubes between communities, and the land that comes back when parking stops.

**[Making & Automation](making.md)** — machines take the repetition, people take the judgement. Repair before manufacture, building to last, and an honest account of what a community cannot make.

**[Knowledge](knowledge.md)** — how practical understanding is kept and passed on when nobody owns it. Also why every page here has a section admitting what it cannot teach.

**[Resource Allocation](allocation.md)** — deciding what goes where without price. Machines count, people decide what matters. The load-bearing page: most other domains defer to it, and it is where the strongest intellectual objections live.

**[Governance](governance.md)** — how decisions get made, and how the arrangement is protected from being quietly reversed. Deliberately short. Most of it is unsolved.

**[Environment](environment.md)** — land, water and living systems as the base everything rests on. Keystone species, soil biology, and giving substantial ground back to itself.

## Not yet written

These domains are referenced throughout and have no page. Any of them is a genuine contribution.

- **Housing & Building** — building for people not yet born. The three-hundred-year principle.
- **Health** — treating the underlying condition rather than the symptoms. Cures versus treatments.
- **Waste** — nothing leaves as waste; everything becomes an input.
- **Livestock** — deliberately unwritten. Communities differ and the arguments are unresolved. Someone who has done it should write this.
- **Transition** — how you get from here to there. The largest gap in the entire project.

## Where the honest gaps are

If you want to know what this project does not know, read the **Unsolved** section of each page. The largest gaps across all domains:

**The industrial base.** No community can make semiconductors, solar panels, batteries or precision machinery. Every community described here depends on them. This is the central unresolved contradiction of the whole project and there is no answer to it here. See [making.md](making.md) and [energy.md](energy.md).

**Scale.** Everything here is demonstrated at community scale or in single domains. Whether any of it composes upward to millions of people is the central open question and is not answered anywhere.

**Transition.** How a society moves from here to there without collapse or coercion. Barely addressed.

**Interfacing with the money world.** A community still needs things it cannot make, from suppliers who want paying. Every real attempt runs a boundary economy and nobody has worked out how to stop the money logic leaking inward.

**Genuinely scarce goods.** Rare minerals, particular medicines, specific land. Allocation without price is hard here and there is no good answer yet.

Contributions that fill these gaps — or that argue honestly that they cannot be filled — are worth more than another page confirming what already works.
